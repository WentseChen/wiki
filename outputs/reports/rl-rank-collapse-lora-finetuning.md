# Research Report: How, When, and Why RL Fine-Tuning Collapses the Rank of LLM Representations — Implications for LoRA-Based RL

**Intersecting Domains:** [[nlp]], [[rl]], [[theory]], [[ml]], [[continuous-learning]]
**Corpus Analyzed:** 24 existing wiki entries + 2 newly ingested bridging papers (DAPO, AEPO).

---

## 1. The Core Intersection (TL;DR)

RL fine-tuning of LLMs — especially under GRPO/RLVR — drives both the *policy distribution* and the *weight-update geometry* toward a small number of dominant modes. At the policy level, this manifests as **entropy collapse**: outputs within a group become near-identical, exploration vanishes, and the policy converges prematurely (per [[dapo-open-source-rl-system]], [[aepo-arbitrary-entropy-policy-optimization]]). At the parameter level, the same dynamics produce **rank collapse**: the effective rank of weight updates shrinks because (a) the policy-gradient covariance becomes low-rank when probability mass concentrates, (b) sparse / binary verifiable rewards produce a low-rank Fisher Information Matrix (per [[rank1-fisher-natural-policy-gradient]]), and (c) the GRPO gradient landscape is 5× flatter across layers than SFT, so per-layer "important directions" are not discriminable (per [[lora-rank-grpo]]).

These are two faces of the same pathology, and they compound a *third*, pre-existing low-rank bias: weight decay + zero-initialization on LoRA's bilinear factorization provably steers stationary points toward low-rank, small-magnitude solutions (per [[lora-convergence-implicit-bias]], [[low-rank-bias-weight-decay-merging]], [[neural-collapse-low-rank-bias]]). LoRA therefore enters RL training with an already rank-capped adapter, then RL further suppresses the few directions that LoRA can express, because LoRA's gradient flow is provably attracted to the dominant singular subspace of the full-FT gradient (per [[lora-one-singular-subspace]]) — and under RL that subspace is itself rank-degenerate. The net effect is a triple bottleneck (LoRA rank cap → RL low-rank gradient → policy entropy collapse) that hurts generalization, increases forgetting, and degrades OOD robustness.

## 2. State of the Art at the Boundary

### 2.1 How rank reduction happens — three converging mechanisms

**(A) Gradient-side, RL-specific.** Bootstrapped value learning regresses onto a moving target generated by the current parameters; iterating this provably shrinks feature-matrix effective rank (per [[implicit-under-parameterization]], formal in linearized regime; empirical across Atari). Capacity loss is the operational handle: the network becomes unable to fit *new* targets even when the old loss is low (per [[capacity-loss-rl]]). In policy-gradient methods, the analog is that the FIM becomes low-rank under sparse rewards because only a few action directions carry any advantage signal (per [[rank1-fisher-natural-policy-gradient]]) — natural-gradient methods covariantly project updates onto this low-rank subspace, accelerating the collapse. In GRPO specifically, the gradient landscape across layers is 5× flatter than SFT (max-to-min layer importance 2.17× vs >10×; top-30% layers carry only 35.7% of signal, vs >80% in SFT), so *gradient amplification feedback* concentrates remaining signal into a few layers (per [[lora-rank-grpo]]).

**(B) Optimization-side, regularization-driven.** Independent of RL, L2 weight decay at gradient-descent stationary points provably produces three coupled properties: parameter-gradient alignment, cross-layer norm preservation, and low-rank bias (per [[low-rank-bias-weight-decay-merging]]). Equivalently, weight decay drives both DNC1 neural collapse and low-rank weight matrices jointly; distance to rank-K is bounded above by total cluster variation divided by the weight-decay coefficient (per [[neural-collapse-low-rank-bias]]). LoRA inherits this: zero-init of adapter B + weight decay creates an implicit bias toward the low-rank global-minimum basin (per [[lora-convergence-implicit-bias]]).

**(C) Policy-side, distributional.** GRPO and RLVR variants exhibit *entropy collapse*: policy entropy decreases monotonically over training, sampled group outputs converge to near-identical strings, and exploration vanishes (per [[dapo-open-source-rl-system]]). Reformulated, this is the *exploration bottleneck* of LLM RL — entropy, exploration, and reward are interconnected, and naive entropy bonuses introduce optimization bias (per [[aepo-arbitrary-entropy-policy-optimization]]). Mechanistically, as the policy sharpens, the score function ∇ log π becomes a near-rank-1 signal on dominant tokens, so the policy gradient ∑ A · ∇ log π lies in a low-rank subspace by construction. This is the upstream cause of (A) in the LLM setting.

### 2.2 When collapse occurs

- **Training stage.** Entropy collapse is observable from the earliest GRPO updates and worsens monotonically (per [[dapo-open-source-rl-system]]); feature-rank collapse in deep RL accelerates with training horizon (per [[implicit-under-parameterization]]).
- **Reward regime.** Sparse / binary verifiable rewards (RLVR) maximize the problem: the Fisher information concentrates on a few "successful" trajectories, yielding low-rank FIM (per [[rank1-fisher-natural-policy-gradient]]); DAPO's dynamic sampling explicitly filters all-correct / all-wrong groups precisely because they provide rank-0 gradient signal (per [[dapo-open-source-rl-system]]).
- **Base model entropy.** Lower base entropy and more aggressive SFT before RL accelerate collapse — RL "corrects SFT-induced directional drift" rather than expanding capacity, and recovery fails under severe SFT overfitting or large distribution shift (per [[rl-vs-sft-singular-vector-analysis]]).
- **LoRA rank choice.** Non-monotonic. Below an optimum rank, in-domain underperforms; above it, knowledge forgetting accelerates with no in-domain gain (per [[lora-rank-tradeoffs-knowledge-robustness]]). The NTK-regime threshold of rank ≳ √N eliminates spurious local minima but does not protect against RL-induced collapse of the *useful* directions within that rank (per [[lora-ntk-no-spurious-minima]]).
- **Learning rate / KL.** [[lora-rlhf-efficiency-regularization]] reports that with LoRA, removing the KL penalty does not hurt performance — interpreted as LoRA's low-rank constraint acting as implicit regularization. The flip side: standard KL is then redundant or weakly opposed to entropy collapse, and is insufficient to prevent reward over-optimization (per [[up-rlhf-diverse-lora-ensembles]]).

### 2.3 Why it hurts generalization

- **Reduced expressivity / feature rank.** Once feature rank is small, the network can only express functions in a small subspace of the target-function space — this is "capacity loss" (per [[capacity-loss-rl]], [[implicit-under-parameterization]]) and "loss of plasticity" (per [[plasticity]], [[plasticity-loss-continual-drl]], [[understanding-plasticity-nn]]). These literatures converge on a single diagnostic — shrinking feature effective rank — with different proximate symptoms (dead units, weight-norm explosion, curvature blow-up).
- **OOD robustness.** Spectral analysis shows direction changes in singular vectors dominate magnitude changes in driving OOD performance; restoring top-20% singular-vector directions recovers 70–80% of OOD perf (per [[rl-vs-sft-singular-vector-analysis]]). Rank collapse rotates and zeros out exactly those directions.
- **Forgetting.** LoRA's "intruder dimensions" — high-rank singular vectors absent in full FT — causally drive forgetting; scaling them down post-hoc restores pre-training knowledge (per [[lora-vs-full-finetuning-illusion]]). Under RL, where updates concentrate further, intruder dimensions can dominate.
- **Mode collapse / diversity loss.** Entropy collapse at the policy level means the model produces a narrow distribution of responses (per [[dapo-open-source-rl-system]], [[aepo-arbitrary-entropy-policy-optimization]]) — quantifiably reduced generation diversity is the consumer-facing symptom.
- **Reward over-optimization / proxy gaming.** A low-rank policy concentrates probability on directions that exploit reward-model errors; the standard KL penalty is insufficient against this without explicit uncertainty quantification (per [[up-rlhf-diverse-lora-ensembles]]).

### 2.4 What this means specifically for LoRA + RL

LoRA in RLHF is widely demonstrated to work well in practice — comparable to full RLHF across 6 datasets with 50–90% memory/speed savings (per [[pe-rlhf-parameter-efficient]]), aligning LLaMA 7B on 2 A100s (per [[lora-rlhf-efficiency-regularization]]), and well-characterized in 300+ PEFT × alignment experiments (per [[peft-preference-alignment-tradeoffs]]). But the *spectral* and *generalization* picture is more delicate:

1. **LoRA's implicit low-rank prior compounds with RL's low-rank gradient.** LoRA's adapter under GD is provably attracted to the singular subspace of the one-step full-FT gradient (per [[lora-one-singular-subspace]]); under RL, that gradient itself has degenerate spectrum. Result: LoRA can converge to a rank far *below* the nominal rank-r ceiling.
2. **Adaptive rank allocation that works under SFT actively hurts under RL.** Proportional rank allocation costs 4.5% accuracy vs uniform on GSM8K under GRPO, because GRPO's flat layer-importance signal is unreliable for allocating capacity — and worse, exhibits gradient-amplification feedback (per [[lora-rank-grpo]]). Uniform rank is the safer default for RL.
3. **Spectral fingerprint of training objective.** The spectral geometry of LoRA deltas (effective rank, stable rank, SV entropy, vector alignment) classifies SFT vs DPO vs steering at AUC ~1.00 (per [[lora-spectral-geometry-training-objective]]), and severity correlates with harmful-compliance ASR (ρ = 0.986) — RL-induced spectral drift is detectable but also predictive of safety drift.
4. **Singular values carry the alignment information.** ESSA reaches GRPO-level alignment by evolutionary search over LoRA singular values alone (per [[essa-evolutionary-strategies-alignment]]); the singular values are the dominant channel where RL writes its update.
5. **LoRA "learns less, forgets less."** The 10–100× lower effective rank of LoRA deltas is the mechanism for both (per [[lora-learns-less-forgets-less]]); under RL this trade-off remains but is reweighted toward more aggressive direction-change in fewer modes.
6. **Routing-level collapse parallels weight-level collapse.** In Mixture-of-LoRAs, learnable routers concentrate on 1–2 adapters (capacity collapse at the router level); fixing it requires non-learnable routing with an RL gradient estimator (per [[remix-rl-routing-mixture-loras]]). Same shape, different mechanism.
7. **Federated/multi-source aggregation is a separate, formal source of collapse.** Heterogeneous-rank client updates with rank-agnostic averaging geometrically suppress higher-rank components — proved, not just observed (per [[fedlora-rank-collapse-prevention]]). For multi-node RL training with mixed-rank LoRA, this is an additional risk.

## 3. Fundamental Gaps & Contradictions

### 3.1 Domain friction

- **"LoRA implicit regularization" vs "LoRA rank collapse."** [[lora-rlhf-efficiency-regularization]] claims LoRA's low-rank constraint already regularizes PPO so the KL penalty is unneeded; [[lora-rank-grpo]] / [[lora-vs-full-finetuning-illusion]] show the same constraint *causes* rank collapse and intruder dimensions. The contradiction is one of framing: a hard rank constraint is both "regularization" (good for KL-style stability) and "capacity collapse" (bad for OOD). The literature has not reconciled when one frame dominates the other.
- **NTK guarantees vs generic-regime reality.** Rank ≳ √N suffices for no spurious minima in the NTK regime (per [[lora-ntk-no-spurious-minima]]); in the generic non-linearized regime, low-rank vs high-rank-spurious is a dichotomy and low-rank usually wins (per [[lora-convergence-implicit-bias]]). The √N prescription is widely cited but its operational meaning under RL — where Lipschitz smoothness fails and convergence is only O(1/log T) (per [[lora-convergence-rate-gd]]) — is unclear.
- **Plasticity-loss vs rank-collapse vs implicit-under-parameterization.** Three RL literatures describe overlapping pathologies with different proximate symptoms: feature rank shrinkage (per [[capacity-loss-rl]], [[implicit-under-parameterization]]), dead units + weight-norm explosion (per [[plasticity]], [[plasticity-loss-continual-drl]]), Hessian curvature blow-up (per [[understanding-plasticity-nn]]). They are not unified theoretically; whether they are facets of a single phenomenon or distinct interacting pathologies remains open.

### 3.2 Unresolved limitations

- **No mechanistic link from policy entropy collapse to weight rank collapse.** The two are observed together in GRPO/RLVR but the formal mapping — "entropy H(π) drops by Δ → effective rank of ΔW drops by f(Δ)" — has not been proved. [[rank1-fisher-natural-policy-gradient]] is the closest: low-rank FIM under sparse rewards implies degenerate natural-gradient updates. But this is a *correlation* in the geometry, not a derivation.
- **LoRA rank-collapse under RL has limited direct empirical evidence.** [[lora-rank-grpo]] studies *layer importance* under GRPO and the failure of *adaptive allocation*, but does not measure the effective rank of the adapter weight deltas as training progresses. The strongest direct evidence remains in the SFT/DPO regime ([[lora-spectral-geometry-training-objective]], [[lora-learns-less-forgets-less]]) and in deep RL value networks ([[implicit-under-parameterization]]) — not directly in LoRA-GRPO weights over training.
- **Generalization metrics are mostly in-domain.** [[lora-learns-less-forgets-less]] reports OOD via forgetting; [[rl-vs-sft-singular-vector-analysis]] uses a card-game OOD shift. Direct measurements of *reasoning generalization* (e.g., AIME → another math benchmark) under rank-collapsed LoRA-RL are absent.
- **What does "rank" of an attention-block delta even mean?** Spectral analyses (per [[lora-spectral-geometry-training-objective]], [[lora-vs-full-finetuning-illusion]]) treat per-layer ΔW separately. But the functional capacity of a transformer is not the sum of per-layer ranks — interactions between QKV projections and MLP rotations couple ranks across layers. No paper in this corpus addresses cross-layer rank.

### 3.3 Contradictory evidence

- **Does LoRA degrade RLHF or not?** [[pe-rlhf-parameter-efficient]] (6 datasets) and [[lora-rlhf-efficiency-regularization]] (LLaMA 7B Alpaca) report comparable or superior alignment to full RLHF. [[lora-learns-less-forgets-less]] (TMLR) reports LoRA "substantially underperforms" full FT in standard low-rank settings on in-domain tasks. Resolution likely depends on (a) base model size, (b) task complexity / required effective rank, (c) rank choice — but a clean predictor of when LoRA is "enough" for RL is missing.
- **Higher rank: helpful or harmful?** [[lora-ntk-no-spurious-minima]] argues for rank ≳ √N; [[lora-rank-tradeoffs-knowledge-robustness]] documents a *non-monotonic* effect where past an optimum, higher rank accelerates forgetting. The optimum is task-dependent and not theoretically characterized.
- **KL needed or not?** [[lora-rlhf-efficiency-regularization]] argues LoRA's implicit regularization makes KL unnecessary; [[up-rlhf-diverse-lora-ensembles]] argues KL is insufficient and explicit uncertainty quantification is needed. These are not directly contradictory (one is about KL's necessity, the other about its sufficiency) but the joint picture — "you can drop KL but you must add ensemble uncertainty" — is not documented as a coherent recipe.

## 4. Proposed Research Angles (Innovation)

### Angle 1 — Joint policy-entropy / weight-effective-rank monitoring during LoRA-RL

**Hypothesis.** During LoRA-GRPO training, policy entropy H(π_t) and effective rank r_eff(ΔW_t) of LoRA deltas decline *jointly* with a phase-lagged correlation, with H(π) leading r_eff(ΔW). A composite "collapse score" (e.g., min(H(π)/H₀, r_eff(ΔW)/r_max)) predicts OOD degradation better than either alone.

**Builds on.** [[lora-rank-grpo]] (RL-specific gradient flatness), [[dapo-open-source-rl-system]] (entropy collapse), [[rank1-fisher-natural-policy-gradient]] (low-rank FIM link), [[lora-spectral-geometry-training-objective]] (per-layer spectral diagnostics).

**Addresses gap.** §3.2's missing mechanistic link from policy entropy collapse to weight rank collapse.

**Experiment sketch.** Train Qwen2.5-1.5B / Llama-3-8B with LoRA-GRPO on GSM8K + a held-out math OOD set. Every K steps, log: (1) policy entropy on a fixed prompt set, (2) effective rank, stable rank, and singular-value entropy of every LoRA ΔA·B (per [[lora-spectral-geometry-training-objective]]'s protocol), (3) in-domain and OOD accuracy. Test whether ablating entropy collapse (via DAPO clip-higher + dynamic sampling) prevents weight-rank collapse, and vice versa via direct spectral regularization.

### Angle 2 — Rank-restoration LoRA via top-k singular-vector replay

**Hypothesis.** Periodically restoring the top-k singular vectors of the base model's W₀ (via low-rank SVD subtraction-and-reprojection of ΔW) prevents intruder-dimension accumulation under sequential / continual LoRA-RL and improves OOD by 5–15%, without hurting in-domain reward.

**Builds on.** [[lora-vs-full-finetuning-illusion]] (intruder dims drive forgetting; scaling them down restores knowledge), [[rl-vs-sft-singular-vector-analysis]] (top-20% directions recover 70–80% OOD), [[capacity-loss-rl]] (InFeR's "stay-near-init" idea), [[goat-lora-adaptive-svd-moe]] (SVD-structured MoE prior).

**Addresses gap.** §3.2's absence of direct LoRA-RL rank-restoration interventions, and §3.3's contradiction on whether higher rank helps — by separating "useful" vs "intruder" rank contributions explicitly.

**Experiment sketch.** Implement a regularizer ‖P_⊥(ΔW)‖² where P_⊥ projects onto the orthogonal complement of the top-k right singular vectors of W₀; tune k vs LoRA rank r vs RL strength. Compare to vanilla LoRA-GRPO and DAPO baselines on math + general-knowledge OOD.

### Angle 3 — Reward-FIM-aware LoRA rank allocation

**Hypothesis.** Per-layer LoRA rank under RL should be allocated proportional to the effective rank of the *layer-wise Fisher Information Matrix* (a quantity that captures both the magnitude of the gradient *and* its directional diversity), not the L2 gradient magnitude that current methods use. This should recover the +4.5% accuracy that uniform allocation has over proportional under GRPO (per [[lora-rank-grpo]]) and also improve OOD.

**Builds on.** [[rank1-fisher-natural-policy-gradient]] (low-rank FIM under sparse rewards; rank-1 approx as compute-cheap proxy), [[lora-rank-grpo]] (gradient L2 is unreliable signal under RL), [[lora-one-singular-subspace]] (LoRA aligns to dominant gradient singular subspace — so allocating rank where the FIM has higher rank is consistent with what LoRA *will actually learn*).

**Addresses gap.** §3.3's contradiction on LoRA rank choice — provides a principled allocator that is RL-aware rather than transplanting SFT heuristics.

**Experiment sketch.** Estimate per-layer FIM effective rank via Hutchinson-style trace + rank-1 perturbations of a vLLM rollout sample. Allocate LoRA rank to layers proportional to FIM effective rank (not L2 norm). Compare to uniform and to L2-proportional baselines under GRPO on GSM8K, MATH, and one OOD reasoning benchmark.

## 6. Concrete Recommendations: Should You Use LoRA for RL Fine-Tuning?

Given the corpus, the answer is a qualified **yes, with safeguards**:

1. **Default to LoRA-PPO/GRPO for memory-/compute-constrained RLHF.** [[pe-rlhf-parameter-efficient]] and [[lora-rlhf-efficiency-regularization]] establish that LoRA is a near-drop-in for full-FT RLHF on standard alignment tasks at the 7B–70B scale with 50–90% resource savings.

2. **Use uniform LoRA rank, not adaptive.** [[lora-rank-grpo]] is unambiguous: gradient-based adaptive rank allocation transferred from SFT loses 4.5% on GSM8K under GRPO. Uniform allocation is the safer default until an RL-aware allocator exists (Angle 3 above).

3. **Choose LoRA rank near the task-specific optimum, not maximally high.** [[lora-rank-tradeoffs-knowledge-robustness]] shows non-monotonicity; the NTK threshold rank ≳ √N (per [[lora-ntk-no-spurious-minima]]) is a *lower* bound for benign optimization, not an upper bound for good generalization. For complex reasoning tasks, rank 32–128 is typical; for narrow alignment, 8–16 may suffice. Sweep this empirically.

4. **Anticipate and instrument for entropy collapse.** Use DAPO-style decoupled clip-higher + dynamic sampling (per [[dapo-open-source-rl-system]]) under GRPO/RLVR. Monitor policy entropy and group-output diversity from step 1; do not wait for benchmark scores to plateau.

5. **Instrument spectral diagnostics on LoRA deltas.** Log effective rank, stable rank, and singular-value entropy of every adapter every K steps (the [[lora-spectral-geometry-training-objective]] protocol). These are cheap and predict harmful-compliance drift.

6. **Avoid sequential / continual LoRA-RL on the same adapter.** Per [[lora-vs-full-finetuning-illusion]], intruder dimensions accumulate across sequential fine-tuning; this is worse under RL where each phase concentrates updates further. Use a fresh adapter per task, or merge-and-restart.

7. **If LoRA underperforms after RL, consider:**
   - **Higher rank** within the [[lora-rank-tradeoffs-knowledge-robustness]] sweep.
   - **MoLF / MoLF-Efficient routing** between LoRA and full FT at the optimizer level (per [[molf-lora-full-finetuning-routing]]).
   - **GOAT-style SVD-MoE over singular-value priors** (per [[goat-lora-adaptive-svd-moe]]).
   - **Post-hoc intruder-dimension scaling** (per [[lora-vs-full-finetuning-illusion]]).
   - **Switching to full-FT RL** if the task requires rank > 10× LoRA's typical setting (the [[lora-learns-less-forgets-less]] finding suggests this for hard in-domain coding/math).

8. **For RLHF reward models, use nuclear-norm-diverse LoRA ensembles** (per [[up-rlhf-diverse-lora-ensembles]]) — this explicitly inverts the rank-collapse concern at the reward-modeling stage and mitigates over-optimization without inflating compute.

9. **Do not rely on KL penalty alone for over-optimization control.** [[lora-rlhf-efficiency-regularization]] reports LoRA's implicit regularization makes KL redundant for stability; [[up-rlhf-diverse-lora-ensembles]] shows KL is insufficient for over-optimization. Add explicit reward-uncertainty penalties.

10. **For continual or multi-task RL, prefer architectures that mitigate rank collapse from the RL side**: CReLU (per [[plasticity-loss-continual-drl]]), continual backprop (per [[plasticity]]), or InFeR-style stay-near-init regularizers (per [[capacity-loss-rl]]) — these target the *RL* mechanism that the LoRA layer cannot itself fix.

## 7. Open Questions & Gaps in the Current Literature
