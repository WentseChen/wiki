# Understanding and Preventing Capacity Loss in Reinforcement Learning

*paper, 2022. Lyle, Rowland, Dabney.*

Formalizes "capacity loss" as the inability of a trained net to fit new targets and ties it to a drop in feature rank caused by non-stationary TD targets. Proposes InFeR — regularize a feature subspace toward initialization — which preserves rank and unlocks sparse-reward Atari (e.g. Montezuma).
